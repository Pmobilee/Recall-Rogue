[h2]THE KNOWLEDGE ROGUELITE[/h2]

Every card in your deck is a real fact. Answer a question to charge it. Charged cards hit harder. Much harder.

Skip the question and play it safe for base damage. Get the answer wrong and the card still fires, just weaker. You're never punished for trying.

The game tracks what you know with FSRS, the same spaced repetition algorithm behind Anki. Facts you struggle with come back more often. Facts you've nailed fade to the back of the queue. You're winning because you're learning. That's the whole trick.

Chain cards from the same topic back-to-back. Two history facts in a row? 1.5x multiplier. Five in a row? 3.5x.

[h2]HOW COMBAT WORKS[/h2]

Five cards per turn. Each one is a mechanic (Strike, Block, Hex, Overclock, Transmute) with a chain color linking it to a topic in your deck. 3 action points.

[b]Quick Play[/b] fires the card instantly. Base damage, no questions asked.

[b]Charge Play[/b] costs an extra action point and pulls up a quiz. Get it right and the card hits harder. Get it wrong and the card still resolves at reduced power.

Three quick plays per turn, or one charged play and one quick. The right call changes every fight.

[h2]THE DUNGEON[/h2]

Three regions. Combat, shops, rest sites, treasure rooms, mystery events. The Burning Library. The Wrong Answer Museum. The Riddle Stranger.

Rest at the campfire. Heal, or study three questions to level up your cards without combat pressure. The Keeper sits by the fire. They've been there longer than you.

[h2]TWO WAYS TO PLAY[/h2]

[b]Trivia Dungeon[/b] The roguelite. Pick a deck, fight through three acts, beat the bosses.

[b]Study Temple[/b] Pick a deck and study it. FSRS-weighted selection focuses on what you're struggling with. Combat is still there, but the emphasis shifts to retention.
