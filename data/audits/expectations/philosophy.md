# philosophy — Expectations

## Intended Scope
History of Western and Eastern philosophy from the pre-Socratics through 20th century analytic and continental traditions. Covers major philosophers, schools, key concepts, famous arguments, and landmark works. Includes Indian and Chinese philosophical traditions.

## Canonical Source
Curated general knowledge, cross-referenced against Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (implicitly). No single canonical source.

## Expected Quality Bar
Highest bar of any deck in this batch — 425 facts, 8 chain themes, well-organized pools. Philosophy terminology can be inherently difficult to quiz (long technical terms, obscure philosopher names).

## Risk Areas
1. **school_names pool LENGTH-TELL** — mix of 6-character and 28-character answers (4.7x ratio) far exceeds the 3x threshold. Short school names (Atomism, Stoicism) will be obvious outliers when distractors are long phrases.
2. **ancient_philosopher_names pool LENGTH-TELL** — "Plato" (5ch) alongside "Diogenes of Sinope" (18ch) (3.6x ratio). At mastery 4 with 5 options, one will be a clear outlier.
3. **concept_terms_medium (113 facts)** — enormous pool; no length further split beyond "medium." Variance within "medium" could still produce length tells.
4. **POOL-CONTAM between philosopher names and concept terms** — some philosopher-name pools may bleed into concept-term questions at mastery 4.
5. **Obscure terminology self-answering** — for very specialized philosophy terms, the question description may contain enough context to make the answer derivable from the question alone.
