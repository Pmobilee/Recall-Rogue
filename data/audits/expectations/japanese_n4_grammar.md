# japanese_n4_grammar — Expectations

## Scope
JLPT N4 grammar — 400 facts. N4 introduces te-form constructions, causative/passive forms, conditional patterns, and hearsay expressions.

## Canonical Source
Standard JLPT N4 grammar reference. Cross-reference: Bunpro N4, jlptsensei.com N4 grammar list.

## Sub-decks
None (flat; 14 semantic pools).

## Pool Inventory
14 pools including: `grammar_all` (400), `conditional` (26), `te_form_giving` (24), `te_form_aspect` (23), `appearance_hearsay` (33), and others.

## Quiz Template
`fill_blank_grammar` exclusively — 100%.

## Quality Bar
- N4 grammar correctly distinguished from N5 (no repetition)
- Causative/passive forms tested in context
- Sentence complexity appropriate for N4 level

## Risk Areas
1. **LENGTH-TELL** — 32 of 90 rows have length-tell options; multi-morpheme answers (なければいけません, させてください) paired with single-morpheme distractors (も, か)
2. **SYNONYM-LEAK for near-identical forms** — なければいけません vs なければならない (both mean "must"); ようです vs みたいです (appearance expressions)
3. **AMBIGUOUS-Q for conditionals** — ば/たら/と/なら all express condition; single-blank context may not uniquely pin one form
4. **Pool size concerns** — some semantic pools small (te_form_aspect: 23); limited distractor variety
5. **Cross-level contamination risk** — grammar patterns from N5 should not appear as correct answers in N4 questions
