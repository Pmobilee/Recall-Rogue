# ap_us_history — Pre-Read Expectations

## 1. Intended Scope
AP U.S. History curriculum, Periods 1–9: colonial era through contemporary America. 9 chain themes map to the 9 AP US History periods. 504 facts with no sub-decks (chainThemes used instead).

## 2. Canonical Source / Exam Alignment
AP U.S. History (College Board). 9 chainThemes correspond to AP APUSH periods. Facts carry `examTags` with Period references.

## 3. Sub-Deck / Chain Theme List
Chain themes (no sub-decks):
- 0: The New World Rift (Pre-Columbian / early contact)
- 1: The Colonial Crucible
- 2: The Liberty Forge (Revolution/Founding)
- 3: The Young Republic (1800–1848)
- 4: The Divided Nation (Civil War era)
- 5: The Iron Colossus (Gilded Age / Reconstruction)
- 6: The Arsenal of Democracy (Progressivism / WWI / Interwar)
- 7: The Atomic Age (WWII / Cold War / Civil Rights)
- 8: The Digital Frontier (1980s–present)

## 5. Expected Quality Bar
Good AP-aligned content. The 9 chain themes are well-named and period-aligned. However, `concept_terms` (142 facts) will produce POOL-CONTAM across all periods.

## 6. Known Risk Areas
1. **`concept_terms` pool at 142 facts** — Covers all 9 APUSH periods; Cold War terms appear as distractors for colonial questions and vice versa. POOL-CONTAM is guaranteed.
2. **`place_names` format issues** — Pool contains annotated names like "Cahokia (Mississippian city)", "Sunbelt (postwar boom region)" — parenthetical annotations may create LENGTH-TELL.
3. **Answer format clutter** — Correct answers in `concept_terms` use parenthetical labels: "Baseball (national pastime)", "CIA covert operations", "Positive good (pro-slavery arg)", "Sharecropping (crop debt cycle)". These are not standard historical term names — they contain editorial labels that may read awkwardly as quiz answers.
4. **Self-answering in `place_names`** — "New York (Dutch colony)" as the correct answer for a question about "renamed New York" — the answer appears in the question ("renamed what colony?").
5. **FACTUAL-SUSPECT** — The Reagan Cold War fact requires verification: Reagan was not president when the USSR dissolved (he left office in 1989; dissolution was 1991 under George H.W. Bush).
