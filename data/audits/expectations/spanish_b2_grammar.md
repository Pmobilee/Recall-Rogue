# spanish_b2_grammar — Expectations

## 1. Intended Scope
CEFR B2 grammar — imperfect subjunctive (both -ra and -se paradigms), compound subjunctive (present and pluperfect with haber), si-clauses Types 2 and 3 (hypothetical and counterfactual), future and conditional perfect, passive voice (ser + PP, se-passive, estar + PP), reported speech with tense shifts, and discourse connectors (concessive, causal, consecutive, temporal, conditional).

## 2. Canonical Source
- Instituto Cervantes PCIC B2 grammatical inventory
- Imperfect subjunctive and mixed si-clauses are defining B2 markers

## 3. Sub-deck / Chain Theme List
8 sub-decks / 8 chain themes:
- Imperfect Subjunctive (ra-form AR, ER/IR, irregulars; se-form parallels)
- Compound Subjunctive (present perfect, pluperfect)
- Si-Clauses: Full System (Type 2, Type 3, mixed)
- Future Perfect & Conditional Perfect
- Passive Voice (3 types)
- Reported Speech
- Discourse Connectors (causal, concessive, consecutive, temporal)
- Por/Para: Advanced Uses

## 4. Answer Pool Inventory
25 pools. All pools meet ≥5 factIds; all padded with exactly 10 synthetic distractors each (highly consistent). This is the most structurally well-configured grammar deck.

## 5. Expected Quality Bar
Highest complexity grammar deck. Fill-in-blank items should clearly require B2 knowledge to disambiguate. Si-clause options must distinguish Type 2 vs Type 3 correctly. Passive voice options must distinguish `ser` vs `estar` vs `se` constructions.

## 6. Risk Areas
1. **Ra vs se paradigm conflation**: Both imperfect subjunctive forms are grammatically valid in standard Spanish. A question filling `hablaRA` in a si-clause could be correctly answered with `hablaRE` (se-form) by a Castilian speaker — minor ambiguity risk.
2. **Reported speech tense shift ambiguity**: Some tense-shift contexts admit multiple valid backshifts; a quiz item could have two defensible correct answers.
3. **Connector pool granularity**: 5 separate connector pools (causal/concessive/consecutive/temporal/conditional) each have only 5 facts — fine-grained but each pool provides minimal within-pool distractor variety before synthetics.
4. **Por vs para at B2**: Advanced por/para uses overlap with B1 level — risk of content duplication with `spanish_b1_grammar` or `spanish_a2_grammar` por/para pool.
5. **No identified quiz failures in sample**: This is the cleanest of all 10 Spanish decks in the 75-item sample — 0 issues detected. Risk areas above are structural/theoretical.
