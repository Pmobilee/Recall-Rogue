# japanese_n2_grammar — Expectations

## Scope
JLPT N2 grammar — 820 facts across 26 semantic categories. N2 grammar is highly formal: compound particles, set expressions, formal speech patterns.

## Canonical Source
Standard JLPT N2 grammar reference (CC BY-SA 4.0).

## Sub-decks
None (flat; 26 semantic pools, 3 facts each — very granular pooling).

## Pool Inventory
26 pools including: `grammar_all` (820), `ability` (8), `addition` (20), `appearance_state` (20), `assertion` (20), `concession_contrast` (20+), etc. Many pools have only ~20 facts.

## Quiz Template
`fill_blank_grammar` exclusively — 100%.

## Quality Bar
- 26 fine-grained pools is architecturally strong — distractors should be semantically coherent within category
- Formal grammar patterns correctly rendered in formal sentence contexts
- Only 9 LENGTH-TELL cases — best grammar deck in this regard

## Risk Areas
1. **LENGTH-TELL** — 9 of 78 rows; still present with compound structures vs short morphemes
2. **Very small pool sizes** — `ability` has only 8 facts; at mastery 4 (needs 4 distractors), may exhaust pool
3. **SYNONYM-LEAK between categories** — N2 has many grammatically similar forms: 〜に基づいて / 〜に従って (both "based on/following"), 〜を通して / 〜を通じて
4. **Formal register mismatch** — some distractors (casual forms) may be clearly eliminable when the question sentence is formal
5. **Pool exhaustion** — tiny pools (ability: 8) at mastery=4 require 4 distractors from only 7 remaining facts; at mastery=2 the pool is not exhausted but may repeat across attempts
