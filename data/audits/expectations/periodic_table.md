# periodic_table — Expectations

## Intended Scope
The 118 elements of the periodic table. Coverage focuses on memorable/testable facts: most abundant elements, highest/lowest properties, discovery stories, symbol origins (especially non-obvious ones like Au, Fe, Pb), elemental categories, and period/group placement.

## Canonical Source
IUPAC element data. Individual element articles on Wikipedia. All data (atomic numbers, symbols, discovery dates) is objectively verifiable.

## Sub-Deck / Chain Theme List
1 sub-deck only ("Elements"). No chain themes populated.

## Answer Pool Inventory
4 pools — minimal but focused:
| Pool | Facts | Synthetics | Notes |
|------|-------|------------|-------|
| element_names | 68 | 0 | Large, no split — but element names are inherently short |
| element_symbols | 8 | 2 | Very small — only 10 total entries |
| element_categories | 5 | 10 | Padded to 15 ✓ |
| bracket_numbers | 9 | 6 | Padded to 15 ✓ |

## Expected Quality Bar
Potentially highest factual reliability of any deck in this batch — element data is fixed and authoritative. Quiz quality risk is low if pools are coherent.

## Risk Areas
1. **element_symbols pool (8 facts + 2 synthetics = 10 total)** — below the recommended 15 minimum. Players will see very repetitive wrong answers for symbol questions.
2. **element_names pool (68 facts, no synthetics)** — no length split but element names are inherently constrained (mostly 4-12 characters). Outliers exist: "Rutherfordium" (13ch) vs "Tin" (3ch) but these are in separate pools by design... or are they?
3. **SELF-ANSWERING** — questions about element discovery/naming often contain derivation hints (e.g., "named after France" → Francium).
4. **No chain themes** — 1 sub-deck and no chain themes. Study Temple mechanic fully non-functional.
5. **FACTUAL-SUSPECT risk low** — all element data is well-established; the main risk is copy errors in specific atomic numbers or discovery years.
