# japanese_n1_grammar — Expectations

## Scope
JLPT N1 grammar — 1,183 facts across 23 semantic categories. N1 grammar is literary, archaic, and highly nuanced: classical forms, set expressions, written-language-only patterns.

## Canonical Source
JLPT N1 Grammar Reference (CC BY-SA 4.0).

## Sub-decks
None (flat; 23 semantic pools, ranging from 12 to 112 facts).

## Pool Inventory
23 pools including: `grammar_all` (1,183), `concession_contrast` (111), `scope_selection` (112), `emphasis` (108), `conditional` (76), `negative_forms` (76), `formal_written` (64), `temporal` (68), etc.

## Quiz Template
`fill_blank_grammar` exclusively — 177 rows sampled, all `fill_blank_grammar`.

## Quality Bar
- N1 patterns are specific and rare; distractors should be from same category
- Literary/formal context requires formal sentence structure throughout
- grammarPointLabel must clearly distinguish near-identical N1 patterns

## Risk Areas
1. **LENGTH-TELL** — 29 of 177 rows; N1 has both short literary particles (つ, とて) and very long compound expressions (とは比べものにならない, 成功しようが失敗しようが)
2. **SYNONYM-LEAK severe** — many N1 patterns are near-synonyms: なり/なりとも, ずとも/すら, をもちまして/をもってすれば
3. **AMBIGUOUS-Q** — at N1 level, multiple grammar patterns can validly fill many blanks; single-blank differentiation is very difficult
4. **purpose pool** — only 12 facts; pool exhaustion at mastery=4
5. **Distractor cross-category pull** — N1 pool breadth means distractors from `scope_selection` (112 facts) may appear in `purpose` questions (12 facts), creating category mismatches
