# Siddhartha Gautama — Verified Source Data
# Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gautama_Buddha
# Fetched: 2026-04-08

## Birth and Early Life
- Born as Siddhartha Gautama in Lumbini (modern-day Nepal)
- Traditional dates: 563–483 BCE (most widely accepted in the Theravada tradition)
- Revisionist dates: 480–400 BCE (based on Indic calendar scholarship)
- Father: Suddhodana — king (or chieftain) of the Shakya clan
- Mother: Maya (also Mayadevi) — died shortly after his birth
- Raised by maternal aunt Mahapajapati Gotami
- Grew up in Kapilavastu, a city in the Shakya kingdom
- Wife: Yashodara (Yasodhara)
- Son: Rahula
- The Shakya clan was a Kshatriya (warrior/ruler) caste group

## The Four Sights
According to Buddhist tradition, Siddhartha was sheltered in the palace from suffering.
On chariot rides outside, he encountered:
1. An old man — revealed the suffering of aging
2. A sick man — revealed the suffering of illness
3. A corpse — revealed the suffering of death
4. An ascetic (wandering holy man) — revealed the possibility of renunciation

## Great Renunciation
- At age 29, Siddhartha left his palace, wife, and newborn son to seek spiritual truth
- This is called the "Great Renunciation" (Mahabhinishkramana)
- He initially studied under two teachers: Alara Kalama and Uddaka Ramaputta
- Then practiced severe asceticism for approximately six years with five companions
- Near starvation, he abandoned extreme asceticism, accepting milk-rice from a girl named Sujata

## Enlightenment
- Siddhartha sat under a Bodhi tree (Ficus religiosa) at Bodh Gaya (modern Bihar, India)
- Attained enlightenment (bodhi) after deep meditation, becoming "the Buddha" (the Awakened One)
- Traditional: attained enlightenment at age 35
- The Bodhi tree he sat under still has a descendant growing at Bodh Gaya today

## First Sermon
- After enlightenment, Buddha traveled to Sarnath (near Varanasi, in Uttar Pradesh, India)
- Delivered his first sermon — the "Dhammacakkappavattana Sutta" (Setting the Wheel of Dharma in Motion)
- Location: the Deer Park (Isipatana) at Sarnath
- Audience: his five former ascetic companions (the "Group of Five")
- This sermon introduced the Four Noble Truths and the Noble Eightfold Path

## Teaching Career
- Traveled and taught for approximately 45 years in the Ganges Plain region
- Founded the Sangha (monastic community of monks and nuns)
- First five disciples became the founding members of the Sangha

## Parinirvana (Death)
- Buddha died at Kushinagar (modern Uttar Pradesh, India)
- Traditional date: ~483 BCE
- Cause: per tradition, eating bad food (possibly poisoned pork or mushrooms) offered by a smith named Cunda
- His final words (according to the Pali Canon): "All conditioned things are impermanent — work out your salvation with diligence."
- His death is called "parinirvana" (final nirvana beyond death)

## Meaning of "Buddha"
- "Buddha" comes from the Sanskrit/Pali root "budh" meaning "to wake" or "to know"
- Literally means "the Awakened One" or "the Enlightened One"
- Not a personal name — Siddhartha's personal name; "Buddha" is a title/epithet
- The "Gautama Buddha" refers to the historical Buddha of this age; Buddhists believe there have been previous Buddhas
