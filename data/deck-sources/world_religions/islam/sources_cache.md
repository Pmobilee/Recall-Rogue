### Muhammad

### Quran
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quran
- 114 surahs (chapters), written in Arabic
- Revealed to Muhammad 610–632 CE
- Compiled under Caliph Uthman ibn Affan (~650 CE)
- Quran means "recitation" in Arabic

### Five Pillars of Islam
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_Pillars_of_Islam
1. Shahada — Declaration of faith ("There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is his messenger")
2. Salat — Five daily prayers (Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha)
3. Zakat — Charitable giving (2.5% of savings above nisab threshold)
4. Sawm — Fasting during Ramadan (9th month of Islamic calendar)
5. Hajj — Pilgrimage to Mecca (required once if able)

### Hadith / Sunnah
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadith
- Hadith: recorded sayings and actions of Muhammad
- Sunnah: Muhammad's example/practice (basis for Islamic law alongside Quran)
- Six canonical Hadith collections (Kutub al-Sittah): Bukhari, Muslim, Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi, Ibn Majah, Al-Nasai

### Islamic Prophets
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prophets_and_messengers_in_Islam
- Islam recognizes 25 prophets explicitly in the Quran
- Muhammad is the "Seal of the Prophets" (Khatam an-Nabiyyin)
- Adam (first human/prophet), Ibrahim (Abraham), Musa (Moses), Isa (Jesus), Muhammad
- Ibrahim considered father of monotheism; ancestor of both Arabs and Jews (via Ishmael and Isaac)

### Angels
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angels_in_Islam
- Jibril (Gabriel): brought the Quran to Muhammad; angel of revelation
- Mikail (Michael): angel of mercy/rain/sustenance
- Israfil: will blow the trumpet on Judgment Day
- Azrael (Malak al-Mawt): angel of death

### Kaaba / Holy Sites
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaaba
- Kaaba: cubic structure in Masjid al-Haram, Mecca; direction of Muslim prayer (qibla)
- Muslims believe Abraham and Ishmael built the Kaaba
- Mecca: holiest city in Islam, birthplace of Muhammad, site of annual Hajj
- Medina: second holiest city; Muhammad's home after Hijra; site of his tomb (Al-Masjid an-Nabawi)
- Jerusalem: third holiest city; Al-Aqsa Mosque; site of Muhammad's Night Journey (Isra and Mi'raj)
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Masjid_al-Haram

### Sunni/Shia Split
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shia%E2%80%93Sunni_relations
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Karbala
- After Muhammad's death (632 CE), dispute over succession
- Sunni: accepted Abu Bakr as first caliph (close companion of Muhammad)
- Shia: believed Ali ibn Abi Talib (Muhammad's cousin and son-in-law) should have succeeded
- Battle of Karbala: 680 CE — Ali's son Husayn ibn Ali and ~72 companions killed by Umayyad forces
- Husayn's martyrdom is central to Shia devotion; commemorated on Ashura (10th of Muharram)
- Shia = ~10-15% of Muslims worldwide; Sunni = ~85-90%

### Caliphates
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashidun_Caliphate
- Rashidun Caliphate: 632–661 CE (Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, Ali) — "rightly guided caliphs"
- Umayyad Caliphate: 661–750 CE (Damascus-based; expanded to Spain/France, Persia, Indus River)
- Abbasid Caliphate: 750–1258 CE (Baghdad; overthrown by Mongol invasion under Hulagu Khan)

### Islamic Golden Age
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Golden_Age
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Wisdom
- House of Wisdom (Bayt al-Hikma): Baghdad; major intellectual center ~8th–13th century
- Al-Khwarizmi (780–850 CE): mathematician; wrote "Al-Kitab al-mukhtasar fi hisab al-jabr wal-muqabala" → origin of the word "algebra"; also source of word "algorithm" (Latinization of his name)
- Ibn Sina / Avicenna (980–1037 CE): physician/philosopher; "The Canon of Medicine" used in European universities until 17th century
- Al-Razi (854–925 CE): physician; first to distinguish smallpox from measles
- Ibn al-Haytham (965–1040 CE): pioneering optics (Book of Optics)

### Sufism
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sufism
- Sufism (Tasawwuf): Islamic mysticism emphasizing inner spiritual dimension and direct experience of God
- Rumi (Jalal ad-Din Muhammad Rumi, 1207–1273 CE): Persian Sufi poet; wrote Masnavi; founded Mevlevi Order (whirling dervishes)
- Al-Ghazali (1058–1111 CE): Islamic theologian/mystic; "Incoherence of the Philosophers"; "Revival of the Religious Sciences"

### Islamic Holidays
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eid_al-Fitr
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eid_al-Adha
- Ramadan: 9th month of Islamic lunar calendar; month of fasting (sawm)
- Eid al-Fitr: festival marking end of Ramadan fasting; "Festival of Breaking the Fast"
- Eid al-Adha: "Festival of the Sacrifice"; commemorates Ibrahim's willingness to sacrifice his son; occurs during Hajj season
- Mawlid al-Nabi: birthday of Muhammad (observed 12th Rabi al-Awwal); not universally celebrated
- Ashura (10th of Muharram): for Shia, commemorates martyrdom of Husayn at Karbala; for Sunni, a day of fasting commemorating Moses's liberation from Egypt

### Mosque Architecture
- Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mosque
- Key elements: minaret (tower for call to prayer), mihrab (niche indicating qibla/direction of Mecca), minbar (pulpit), wudu area (ritual washing)
- Madrasa: Islamic educational institution traditionally attached to a mosque
- Adhan: call to prayer announced from minaret
