## 1. Beer Hall Putsch (1923)

**Wikipedia URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beer_Hall_Putsch
**USHMM URL:** https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/beer-hall-putsch-munich-putsch

### Exact Dates
- **8–9 November 1923** — the attempted coup
- **26 February – 1 April 1924** — Hitler's treason trial (24 days)
- **1 April 1924** — Hitler sentenced
- **20 December 1924** — Hitler released from Landsberg Prison (after serving 9 months)

### Key Names
**People:**
- Adolf Hitler (leader of the putsch)
- Erich Ludendorff (co-leader; WWI general lending legitimacy)
- Gustav Ritter von Kahr (Bavarian State Commissioner; initially seized at gunpoint, later reversed his support)
- Otto von Lossow (Bavarian Reichswehr commander)
- Hans Ritter von Seisser (Bavarian State Police chief)
- Rudolf Hess (fellow prisoner; helped dictate *Mein Kampf*)
- Hermann Göring (participated; wounded)
- Heinrich Himmler (participated)
- Julius Streicher (participated)
- Wilhelm Frick (participated)

**Places:**
- Bürgerbräukeller — the beer hall in Munich where the putsch began (Hitler jumped on a table and fired a pistol into the ceiling)
- Feldherrnhalle (Hall of Commanders), Odeonsplatz — where the march was stopped and shots were fired
- Landsberg am Lech — prison where Hitler was held

**Organizations:**
- Nazi Party (NSDAP)
- Kampfbund (Combat League) — coalition of right-wing paramilitary groups
- Weimar Republic

### Key Numbers
- ~2,000 Nazis and Kampfbund members marched toward the city center
- **14 Nazis and 4 police officers killed** (some sources cite 16 Nazis; ~18–20 total deaths)
- ~35–100+ wounded (sources vary)
- **5-year sentence** handed to Hitler (minimum under treason law)
- **9 months actually served** (264 days)
- Nazi Party had ~50,000+ members by 1923

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions
1. Hitler was convicted of **high treason** — yet received the minimum possible sentence of 5 years due to a sympathetic, nationalist-leaning judge.
2. Hitler served only **9 months** of his 5-year sentence before being paroled on 20 December 1924.
3. The putsch initially appeared to succeed when Hitler forced the three Bavarian leaders (Kahr, Lossow, Seisser) at gunpoint to join him — but all three reversed their support once free from direct threat.
4. The failure directly caused Hitler to abandon violent revolution in favor of seeking power through **legal electoral means**.

### Commonly Confused / Surprising
- The putsch was a **complete failure** militarily and politically, yet it gave Hitler national publicity via his widely-reported trial, which he used as a propaganda platform.
- Ludendorff, the WWI hero, walked through the police gunfire line and was not arrested immediately — his prestige temporarily shielded him.
- Prison authorities allowed Hitler **civilian clothes, letter privileges, visitors, and his personal secretary** — conditions that enabled him to dictate *Mein Kampf*.

### Notable Quotes
- Hitler at trial: Used the platform to denounce the "November criminals" and the Weimar Republic, turning defeat into a propaganda victory. (Exact trial quotes UNVERIFIED — needs primary source check.)

- **Franz von Papen** — former Chancellor; convinced Hindenburg to appoint Hitler; became Vice-Chancellor believing he could control Hitler
- **Kurt Baron von Schröder** — Cologne banker at whose house Hitler and Papen held a secret meeting on **4 January 1933**
- **Alfred Hugenberg** — leader of the German National People's Party (DNVP); key coalition partner
- **Kurt von Schleicher** — Hitler's immediate predecessor as Chancellor (resigned 28 January 1933)

### Key Numbers

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions

### Key Names
**Arsonist:**
- **Marinus van der Lubbe** — Dutch communist; caught at the scene with fire-starting materials; arrested, tried, convicted, and **executed on 10 January 1934**

**Politicians who exploited the fire:**
- Adolf Hitler (Chancellor)

- Joseph Goebbels (Propaganda Minister)

**Acquitted defendants (communist "co-conspirators"):**
- Ernst Torgler (German Communist Party parliamentary leader)

- Vassili Tanev (Bulgarian)

### Key Numbers
- **27 February 1933** — fire

- **5 March 1933** — Reichstag elections (last multi-party election until after WWII)

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions
1. The fire occurred **just 4 weeks** after Hitler became Chancellor, and he used it immediately to claim communist revolution was imminent.
2. All **four communist co-defendants were acquitted** at the Leipzig trial — embarrassing the Nazi government — yet van der Lubbe was still executed.
3. The Reichstag Fire Decree (28 February 1933) suspended **Articles 114, 115, 117, 118, 123, 124, and 153** of the Weimar Constitution, effectively eliminating civil liberties. (UNVERIFIED on exact article numbers — needs check.)

### Commonly Confused / Surprising
- Whether the Nazis **themselves set the fire** remains historically debated; most historians now believe van der Lubbe acted alone, but the Nazis almost certainly exploited the event opportunistically.
- Van der Lubbe was tried under a law passed **after** the fire — a legal retroactivity that was itself controversial.
- Georgi Dimitrov's acquittal made him an international hero of anti-fascism; he later became leader of Communist Bulgaria.

## 5. Enabling Act (23 March 1933)

**Source URLs:**
- https://www.britannica.com/topic/Enabling-Act
- https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/the-enabling-act
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enabling_Act_of_1933

### Exact Date
- **23 March 1933** — passed by the Reichstag (some sources say signed/enacted 24 March; 23 March is the vote date)

### Full Official Name
- German: **Gesetz zur Behebung der Not von Volk und Reich**
- English: **"Law to Remedy the Distress of the People and the Reich"**
- Informally known as the Enabling Act (*Ermächtigungsgesetz*)

### Vote Counts
- **441 votes FOR** (including Nazis, DNVP, Centre Party, other centre-right parties)
- **94 votes AGAINST** (Social Democrats only)
- **0 Communist votes** — Communist deputies had already been arrested or were in hiding after the Reichstag Fire Decree

### Parties and Their Positions
- **Nazi Party (NSDAP):** Yes — 288 seats
- **German National People's Party (DNVP):** Yes — coalition partner
- **Centre Party (Zentrum):** Yes — voted yes after Hitler's verbal promises to protect Catholic schools and the Concordat (promises he later broke)
- **Social Democrats (SPD):** **NO** — the only party to vote against
- **Communists (KPD):** Excluded — banned after Reichstag fire

### Powers Granted
- Allowed Hitler's cabinet to **pass laws without Reichstag approval** for 4 years
- Laws could **deviate from the Weimar Constitution**
- **Transferred all legislative power** to the executive (chancellor + cabinet)
- Hitler could now rule by **decree** without parliamentary oversight

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions
1. The Enabling Act passed by **441 to 94** — the only party to vote against it was the **Social Democrats (SPD)**.
2. The full official German name was **"Gesetz zur Behebung der Not von Volk und Reich"** — "Law to Remedy the Distress of the People and the Reich."
3. The Catholic **Centre Party** voted yes after Hitler made verbal promises to protect Catholic institutions — promises he subsequently ignored.

### Commonly Confused / Surprising
- Hitler obtained dictatorial power through an **ostensibly legal parliamentary vote**, not a coup — he maintained the facade of constitutional legality.
- A **two-thirds supermajority** was required; the Nazis only had ~44% of seats. They achieved it by excluding Communists and winning over the Centre Party.
- SPD leader Otto Wels gave a famous speech opposing the act — the only voice of resistance in the chamber that day. (Exact quote UNVERIFIED — needs primary source check.)

### Exact Dates

- **30 June – 2 July 1934** — main killing period
- **1 July 1934** — Ernst Röhm executed by two SS guards

### Key Names
**Perpetrators:**
- Adolf Hitler (ordered the purge)
- Heinrich Himmler (SS; provided the execution squads)
- Hermann Göring (co-instigator; urged Hitler to act)
- Reinhard Heydrich (SS intelligence; compiled the target lists)

**Major Victims:**
- **Ernst Röhm** — Chief of Staff of the SA (Sturmabteilung/Brownshirts); Hitler's longtime ally; executed 1 July 1934
- **Kurt von Schleicher** — former Chancellor of Germany; shot at his home
- **Gregor Strasser** — former Nazi Party organization chief and socialist-leaning rival to Hitler
- **Gustav von Kahr** — former Bavarian State Commissioner (the same man who betrayed Hitler in 1923); killed in revenge
- **Edgar Jung** — conservative intellectual; speechwriter for Papen
- **Erich Klausener** — Catholic Action leader
- **Franz von Papen** — Vice-Chancellor; narrowly escaped death; dismissed 3 July 1934; his staff were killed

**Organization Targeted:**
- SA (Sturmabteilung) — the Nazi paramilitary "Brownshirts"; ~2–3 million members at peak

### Key Numbers
- Hitler officially claimed: **61 executed, 13 shot resisting arrest, 3 committed suicide** = 77 official
- Conservative historical estimate: **at least 85 killed** (Heinrich Bennecke)
- Higher estimates: **150–over 1,000** killed (sources vary widely; exact number obscured by Nazi cover-up)
- SA at its peak had approximately **2–3 million members**

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions
1. Hitler personally arrested SA Chief Ernst **Röhm** at a hotel in **Bad Wiessee**, Bavaria, at approximately 6 AM on 30 June 1934.
2. Among those killed was **Kurt von Schleicher** — the man who had been Chancellor immediately before Hitler — shot at his own home along with his wife.
3. **Gustav von Kahr** — who had betrayed Hitler during the 1923 Beer Hall Putsch — was among those murdered, demonstrating Hitler settling old scores.

### Commonly Confused / Surprising
- The "Night" actually lasted **three days** (30 June – 2 July).
- The purge was not limited to SA leaders — Hitler used it to eliminate **personal enemies and political rivals** across factions, including conservatives like Schleicher and Strasser.
- The German army generals **approved** of the purge; they had long resented the SA threatening to absorb them. Their support was the key political prize Hitler gained.
- The SA (Brownshirts) had been the muscle of the Nazi movement since the 1920s; after the purge, their power was permanently broken and the SS (Schutzstaffel) became dominant.

### Exact Dates
- **1 August 1934** — Cabinet passes the "Law Concerning the Head of State of the German Reich" (effective upon Hindenburg's death)
- **2 August 1934** — President Paul von Hindenburg dies at 9:00 AM; Hitler automatically assumes merged office
- **19 August 1934** — German plebiscite confirms Hitler's new title (UNVERIFIED on exact plebiscite result percentage — needs manual check)

### Title and Meaning
- Official title: **Führer und Reichskanzler** ("Leader and Reich Chancellor")
- *Führer* (German) = "Leader" or "Guide"
- The title merged both the **presidency** (head of state) and the **chancellorship** (head of government) into a single position held by Hitler alone

### Key Names
- **Paul von Hindenburg** — President whose death triggered the merger; died 2 August 1934
- **Adolf Hitler** — assumed the merged office
- **Heinrich Himmler** — *Reichsführer-SS*; head of the SS, now reporting directly to Hitler as supreme authority

### The Military Oath
On **2 August 1934**, the entire German armed forces swore a new personal oath — **not to Germany or the constitution, but to Adolf Hitler personally**:
> "I swear by God this sacred oath, that I will render unconditional obedience to Adolf Hitler, the Führer of the German Reich and people, Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces..." (UNVERIFIED exact wording — primary source recommended)

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions
1. Hitler merged the offices of **Chancellor and President** on **2 August 1934**, the day President Hindenburg died — a law enabling this had been passed by cabinet **the day before** (1 August).
2. The German army swore a **personal oath of loyalty to Hitler** (not to Germany or the constitution) on the day Hindenburg died.
3. A plebiscite held on **19 August 1934** confirmed Hitler's new title — with the Nazi regime reporting ~89.9% approval (UNVERIFIED exact figure — needs manual check).

### Commonly Confused / Surprising
- The merger was **pre-arranged**: Hitler's cabinet passed the enabling law on 1 August, the day *before* Hindenburg actually died — technically before the office was even vacant.
- The title *Führer* had been used informally within the Nazi Party since the early 1920s; this was its official adoption for the whole German state.
- The 43 territorial administrators under Hitler were called ***Gauleiter*** ("district leaders").

## 8. Nuremberg Laws (15 September 1935)

**Source URLs:**
- https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/nuremberg-laws
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuremberg_Laws
- https://hmd.org.uk/resource/15-september-1935-introduction-of-the-nuremberg-laws/
- https://museums.nuernberg.de/documentation-center/topics/national-socialism/racism-and-anti-semitism/the-nuremberg-laws

### Exact Dates
- **10–16 September 1935** — Seventh Annual Nazi Party Rally, Nuremberg
- **15 September 1935** — Both Nuremberg Laws passed unanimously by the Reichstag in a special session held in Nuremberg (the **only Reichstag session held outside Berlin** during the Nazi era)
- **14 November 1935** — First Supplementary Decree: defined who was legally considered Jewish; brought the Reich Citizenship Law into effect
- **26 November 1935** — Further regulations extended measures to "Gypsies, Negroes, and their bastards"
- **1 January 1936** — Article 3 of the Blood Law came into force

### The Two Laws

**Law 1: Reich Citizenship Law** (*Reichsbürgergesetz*)
- Declared that only persons of "German or related blood" could be German citizens
- Jews and others became mere "state subjects" (*Staatsangehörige*) without full citizenship rights

**Law 2: Law for the Protection of German Blood and German Honor** (*Gesetz zum Schutze des deutschen Blutes und der deutschen Ehre*)
- Prohibited **marriage** between Jews and non-Jewish Germans
- Prohibited **sexual relations** outside marriage between Jews and non-Jewish Germans ("race defilement" / *Rassenschande*)
- Prohibited Jews from **employing female German maids** under 45 years of age
- Prohibited Jews from **displaying the German flag**

### Definition of "Jewish" (14 November 1935 decree)
- **3 or 4 Jewish grandparents** = classified as Jewish under law (regardless of personal religion or practice)
- **2 Jewish grandparents** = *Mischling* ("mixed-blood") first degree — some restrictions
- **1 Jewish grandparents** = *Mischling* second degree — fewer restrictions
- This definition caught people who had **converted to Christianity** or had no Jewish cultural identity

### Key Numbers
- Passed **15 September 1935**
- Supplementary decree **14 November 1935**
- Reichstag vote: **unanimous** (no dissent recorded)
- Approximately **500,000 Jews** in Germany at the time (UNVERIFIED exact figure — needs manual check)

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions
1. The Nuremberg Laws were announced at the **7th Annual Nazi Party Rally** on **15 September 1935** — the only time the Reichstag convened **outside Berlin** under Nazi rule.
2. The definition of "Jewish" was based **solely on ancestry** (grandparents born into the Jewish religious community), not on personal religious practice — catching many who had converted to Christianity.
3. The Law for the Protection of German Blood banned **marriages between Jews and non-Jewish Germans** and prohibited Jews from employing **German women under 45** as domestic servants.

### Commonly Confused / Surprising
- The laws were based on **ancestry, not religion** — people who had never practiced Judaism and had converted to Christianity were still classified as Jewish if they had 3+ Jewish grandparents.
- The laws were announced **at a Nazi rally** in Nuremberg, not in Berlin — they were designed as a propaganda spectacle.
- Italy, Hungary, Romania, and Vichy France subsequently enacted **similar racial legislation** by 1941.

## 9. Nazi Propaganda Machine (Goebbels, Book Burnings)

**Source URLs:**
- https://www.britannica.com/biography/Joseph-Goebbels
- https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/book-burning
- https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/americanexperience/features/goebbels-burnings/
- https://germanhistory-intersections.org/en/knowledge-and-education/ghis:document-158

### Goebbels: Key Facts

**Full Name:** Paul Joseph Goebbels
**Born:** 29 October 1897, Rheydt, Germany
**Died:** 1 May 1945, Berlin (suicide)

**Title and Role:**
- From **13 March 1933**: **Reich Minister for Public Enlightenment and Propaganda**
- German: *Reichsminister für Volksaufklärung und Propaganda* (RMVP)
- At age **35**, he was the **youngest minister** in Hitler's cabinet

**Earlier Party Roles:**
- **November 1926**: Appointed *Gauleiter* (district leader) of Berlin by Hitler
- **1928**: Appointed head of Nazi Party propaganda nationwide (*Reichspropagandaleiter*)

**What His Ministry Controlled:**
The RMVP controlled all German media and culture:
- Press / newspapers
- Radio (crucial — the Volksempfänger "people's receiver" radio sets were mass-produced)
- Cinema and film
- Theater
- Literature
- Music
- Fine arts
The ministry also created the **Reichskulturkammer** (Reich Chamber of Culture), which required all artists and media workers to be members — and could exclude Jews and political opponents.

**Personal Details (quiz-worthy):**
- Had a **clubfoot** (likely from childhood polio/osteomyelitis) which exempted him from WWI service
- Previously had Jewish friends and a romantic relationship with a half-Jewish woman before his radicalization
- Became **Chancellor of Germany for a single day** (1 May 1945) before committing suicide along with his wife Magda (who also killed their six children)

### Book Burnings: Key Facts

**Date:** **10 May 1933** (primary events; additional burnings in late June 1933)

**Scale:**
- Occurred in **more than 20 university towns** across Germany (some sources say up to 34 cities)
- Organized by the *Deutsche Studentenschaft* (DSt) — pro-Nazi university students' union
- **~40,000 people** gathered at the main Berlin event at **Opernplatz** (Opera Square, now Bebelplatz)
- Approximately **20,000 volumes** burned at Berlin alone
- **Over 25,000 books** burned nationally on that night

**Goebbels' Berlin Speech (10 May 1933):**
> "The era of extreme Jewish intellectualism is now at an end... And thus you do well in this midnight hour to commit to the flames the evil spirit of the past."
> — Joseph Goebbels, Opernplatz, Berlin, 10 May 1933

**Books / Authors Targeted:**
- Sigmund Freud (psychoanalysis)
- Albert Einstein (physics / Jewish science)
- Erich Maria Remarque (*All Quiet on the Western Front* — pacifism)
- Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels (communism)
- Heinrich Heine (Jewish poet, 19th century)
- Kurt Tucholsky and Carl von Ossietzky (Nazi critics)
- Bertolt Brecht (leftist playwright)
- Ernest Hemingway and Helen Keller (American authors labeled "un-German")

**The Heine Prophecy:**
Heinrich Heine — one of the **authors whose books were burned** — had written in his 1820/1821 play *Almansor*:
> "Dort wo man Bücher verbrennt, verbrennt man am Ende auch Menschen."
> ("Where they burn books, they will ultimately burn people too.")
— Heinrich Heine, *Almansor* (1821)
This quote is displayed today at the memorial at Bebelplatz in Berlin.

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions
1. The Reich Ministry for Public Enlightenment and Propaganda was established on **13 March 1933** — just **6 weeks** after Hitler became Chancellor.
2. Goebbels delivered his book-burning speech to **~40,000 people** at Berlin's **Opernplatz** on **10 May 1933**; the event was broadcast on national radio.
3. Heinrich Heine — one of the poets whose works were burned — wrote in 1821: **"Where they burn books, they will ultimately burn people too"** — a quote now memorialized at the site of the Berlin burning.

### Commonly Confused / Surprising
- The book burnings were **organized by university students**, not the Nazi government directly — though government officials attended and celebrated them.
- Goebbels was **not initially** a committed antisemite; he had Jewish friends and a Jewish girlfriend in his early years.
- The **Volksempfänger** ("people's receiver") radio was a cheap, state-subsidized radio set designed specifically to ensure all German households could receive Nazi broadcasts — one of the most sophisticated propaganda tools in history.

## 10. Hitler Youth (*Hitlerjugend*)

**Source URLs:**
- https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/hitler-youth-2
- https://www.britannica.com/topic/Hitler-Youth
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hitler_Youth
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baldur_von_Schirach

### Founding and Key Dates
- **March 1922** — Founded as *Jugendbund der NSDAP* (Youth League of the NSDAP)
- **April 1924** — Renamed *Grossdeutsche Jugendbewegung* (Greater German Youth Movement) while Nazi Party was banned
- **4 July 1926** — Officially renamed **Hitler-Jugend, Bund der deutschen Arbeiterjugend** (Hitler Youth, League of German Worker Youth) — this is the formal founding date most often cited
- **1931** — Baldur von Schirach appointed first *Reichsjugendführer* (Reich Youth Leader)
- **1933** — Massive expansion after Nazi takeover; competing youth organizations dissolved
- **1 December 1936** — "Law on the Hitler Youth" passed; membership made **compulsory** in law for all "Aryan" German youth
- **1 July 1936** — Hitler Youth became a state agency (some sources cite this date for state incorporation)
- **1939** — Membership made **obligatory** in practice (enforcement tightened)
- **1945** — Organization dissolved at end of war

### Key Names
- **Baldur von Schirach** — *Reichsjugendführer* (Reich Youth Leader), 1931–1940; later Gauleiter of Vienna; convicted at Nuremberg
- **Artur Axmann** — Replaced Schirach as Reich Youth Leader in 1940

### Structure and Age Groups

| Organization | Gender | Ages |
|---|---|---|
| *Deutsches Jungvolk* (DJ) | Boys | 10–14 |
| *Hitlerjugend* (HJ) proper | Boys | 14–18 |
| *Jungmädel* (JM) | Girls | 10–14 |
| *Bund Deutscher Mädel* (BDM) — League of German Girls | Girls | 14–18 |

After age 18: boys entered the Nazi Party, Reich Labour Service (*Reichsarbeitsdienst*), then the armed forces.

### Membership Numbers
- **1932**: ~107,000 members (UNVERIFIED — needs manual check)
- **1933**: Rapid expansion after Nazi takeover
- **1935**: Nearly **60% of all German boys** were members
- **1936**: Membership made compulsory by law
- **1939**: ~8 million members (UNVERIFIED — needs manual check)
- **1940**: Virtually all eligible German youth enrolled

### Notable Facts for Quiz Questions
1. The Hitler Youth was originally founded in **March 1922** (over a decade before Hitler came to power) and received its formal name on **4 July 1926**.
2. Membership became **legally compulsory** for all "racially pure" German youth in December 1936 via the "Law on the Hitler Youth."
3. Boys aged **10–14** were in the *Deutsches Jungvolk*; the *Hitlerjugend* proper was for boys **14–18**. Girls had parallel organizations: the *Jungmädel* (10–14) and *Bund Deutscher Mädel* (14–18).

### Commonly Confused / Surprising
- The organization was **not** founded in 1933 — it predated Hitler's chancellorship by over a decade.
- The Hitler Youth was comprehensive **ideological indoctrination**, not just military training: it controlled schooling, leisure, physical fitness, and values — designed to replace family and church as primary sources of loyalty.
- Registration at age 10 included an investigation specifically for **"racial purity"** — Jewish children were excluded.
- Girls' organizations trained for **domestic duties and motherhood**, not combat — their indoctrination emphasized producing the next generation of "Aryan" Germans.
