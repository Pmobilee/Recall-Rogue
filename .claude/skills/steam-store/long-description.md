## Readable version (for review)

### THE KNOWLEDGE ROGUELITE

Every card in your deck is a real fact. Charge it by answering a question — the card hits harder. Much harder. Skip the question and play it safe for base damage. Get the answer wrong and the card still fires, just weaker. You're never punished for trying.

The game tracks what you know with FSRS — the same spaced repetition algorithm behind Anki. Facts you struggle with come back more often. Facts you've nailed fade to the back of the queue. You're winning because you're learning. That's the whole trick.

Knowledge Chains reward you for charging cards from the same topic back-to-back. Two history facts in a row? 1.5x multiplier. Five in a row? 3.5x. The dungeon has teeth, but so does knowing things.

### HOW COMBAT WORKS

You draw five cards per turn. Each one is a mechanic — Strike, Block, Hex, Overclock, Transmute — with a chain color linking it to a topic in your deck. You have 3 action points.

**Quick Play** — tap the card, it fires instantly. Base damage, no questions asked.

**Charge Play** — costs an extra action point and pulls up a quiz question. Answer correctly and the card hits harder. Answer wrong and the card still resolves at reduced power.

Three quick plays per turn, or one charged play and one quick. The right call changes every fight.

### ENEMIES THAT CARE WHAT YOU KNOW

89 enemies across three dungeon regions. Some react directly to your quiz performance.

The Peer Reviewer gains strength every turn you don't charge. Pop Quiz is stunned by correct answers but grows if you play it safe. The Curriculum becomes immune to quick plays halfway through — only charged knowledge gets through. The Crib Sheet mirrors your wrong-answer damage back at you.

8 bosses, each with phase transitions. The Final Lesson tests you with rapid-fire quiz rounds at 66% and 33% HP — wrong answers power it up.

### 98 DECKS. 67,000 FACTS.

Pick your deck before each run. Every deck is a curated set of real knowledge.

**History & Culture** — Ancient Greece, Roman Empire, World War II, Medieval World, US Presidents, AP World History, Philosophy, World Religions

**Science & Nature** — Periodic Table, AP Biology, AP Chemistry, AP Physics, Dinosaurs, Ocean Life, Constellations, NASA Missions

**Languages** — Japanese (JLPT N5-N1), Chinese (HSK 1-6), Spanish (A1-C2), French (A1-B2), German, Korean, Czech, Dutch

**Games & Puzzles** — 620,000+ chess tactics puzzles with Elo rating, interactive board, no multiple choice

**And more** — Famous Paintings, World Flags, Medical Terminology, Pharmacology, Computer Science, Pop Culture, Movies & Cinema, Music History, AP Psychology, AP Economics

### CARDS, RELICS, CHAINS

**38+ card mechanics** across 6 types. Strikes, shields, hexes, buffs, debuffs, and wild cards — each with mastery levels 0-5 that scale as you answer correctly.

**56 relics** that shape your build. Quicksilver Quill rewards fast answers with bonus damage. Scholar's Crown amplifies cards from your review queue. Volatile Core makes every charge hit 50% harder — but wrong answers hurt you. Double Down lets you take two quizzes on one card for 5x power, once per fight.

**Knowledge Chains** — 3 chain colors per run, drawn from your deck's topics. Charge same-color cards in sequence for multipliers up to 3.5x.

**20 ascension levels** for when the base game stops hurting enough.

### THE DUNGEON

Three regions. Combat, shops, rest sites, treasure rooms, mystery events. The Burning Library. The Wrong Answer Museum. The Riddle Stranger.

Rest at the campfire. Heal, or study three questions without combat pressure to level up your cards for free. The Keeper sits by the fire. They've been here longer than you have.

### TWO WAYS TO PLAY

**Trivia Dungeon** — the roguelite. Pick a deck, fight through three acts, beat the bosses.

**Study Temple** — pick a deck and study it. FSRS-weighted fact selection focuses on what you're struggling with. Combat is still there, but the emphasis shifts to retention.
