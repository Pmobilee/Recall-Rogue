## Readable version (for review)

### THE KNOWLEDGE ROGUELITE

Every card in your deck is a real fact. Play it safe for base damage, or charge it by answering a question for a massive power spike. Get it right and the card erupts. Get it wrong and it still fires — just weaker. No card is ever wasted.

Knowledge Chains reward you for charging cards from the same topic back-to-back. Two history facts in a row? 1.5x multiplier. Five in a row? 3.5x. The dungeon has teeth, but so does knowing things.

### HOW COMBAT WORKS

You draw five cards per turn. Each one is a mechanic — Strike, Block, Hex, Overclock, Transmute — with a chain color linking it to a topic in your deck. You have 3 action points.

**Quick Play** — tap the card, it fires instantly. Base damage, no questions asked. Fast, reliable, safe.

**Charge Play** — costs an extra action point and pulls up a quiz question from your deck. Answer correctly and the card hits harder. Much harder. Answer wrong and the card still resolves at reduced power. You're never punished for trying.

The tradeoff is real. Three quick plays per turn, or one charged play and one quick. The right call changes every fight.

### ENEMIES THAT CARE WHAT YOU KNOW

89 enemies across three dungeon regions. Some of them react directly to your quiz performance.

The Peer Reviewer gains strength every turn you don't charge. Pop Quiz is stunned by correct answers but grows if you play it safe. The Curriculum becomes immune to quick plays halfway through the fight — only charged knowledge gets through. The Crib Sheet mirrors your wrong-answer damage back at you.

8 bosses, each with phase transitions. The Final Lesson tests you with rapid-fire quiz rounds at 66% and 33% HP — wrong answers power it up.

### 98 DECKS. 67,000 FACTS.

Pick your deck before each run. Every deck is a curated set of real knowledge.

**History & Culture** — Ancient Greece, Roman Empire, World War II, Medieval World, US Presidents, AP World History, Philosophy, World Religions

**Science & Nature** — Periodic Table, AP Biology, AP Chemistry, AP Physics, Dinosaurs, Ocean Life, Constellations, NASA Missions

**Languages** — Japanese (JLPT N5-N1), Chinese (HSK 1-6), Spanish (A1-C2), French (A1-B2), German, Korean, Czech, Dutch — 7 languages with proficiency levels from first words to fluency

**Games & Puzzles** — 620,000+ chess tactics puzzles with Elo rating, interactive board, no multiple choice

**And more** — Famous Paintings, World Flags, Medical Terminology, Pharmacology, Computer Science, Pop Culture, Movies & Cinema, Music History, AP Psychology, AP Economics

The game uses FSRS — the same spaced repetition algorithm behind Anki. It tracks what you know and what you don't, across runs. Facts you struggle with come back more often. Facts you've nailed fade to the back of the queue. You're actually learning, not just grinding.

### CARDS, RELICS, CHAINS

**38+ card mechanics** across 6 types. Strikes, shields, hexes, buffs, debuffs, and wild cards — each with mastery levels 0-5 that scale up as you answer correctly.

**56 relics** that shape your build. Quicksilver Quill rewards fast answers with bonus damage. Scholar's Crown amplifies cards from your review queue. Volatile Core makes every charge hit 50% harder — but wrong answers hurt you. Double Down lets you take two quizzes on one card for 5x power, once per fight.

**Knowledge Chains** — 3 chain colors per run, drawn from your deck's topics. Charge same-color cards in sequence for multipliers up to 3.5x. A chain of Founding Fathers questions stacks differently than a chain of Civil War facts, even in the same US Presidents deck.

### THE DUNGEON

Three regions, each deeper and harder. Combat encounters, shops, rest sites, treasure rooms, and mystery events — the Burning Library, the Wrong Answer Museum, the Riddle Stranger.

Rest at the campfire. Heal, or study three questions without combat pressure to level up your cards for free. The Keeper sits by the fire. They've been here longer than you have.

20 ascension levels for when the base game stops hurting enough. Higher ascensions tighten the timer, add elite encounters, make wrong answers deal self-damage — but also hand you stronger starting relics and rarer card options.

### TWO WAYS TO PLAY

**Trivia Dungeon** — the roguelite. Pick a deck, fight through three acts, beat the bosses. Broad fact selection, no repeats, difficulty scales with depth.

**Study Temple** — pick a deck and study it. FSRS-weighted fact selection that focuses on what you're struggling with. The card combat is still there, but the emphasis shifts to retention.
